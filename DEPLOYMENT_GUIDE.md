## 🎯 INTERVIEW PREPARATION

### Before Any Interview, Do This:

#### 1. Know Your Projects Inside-Out
```
For each project, be able to explain:
- Problem (business challenge)
- Approach (your solution)
- Code (key parts)
- Results (metrics)
- What you learned
- What you'd do differently

Use: PROJECT_DOCUMENTATION.md for details
```

#### 2. Practice Common Questions
```
Use: RECRUITER_OPTIMIZATION_GUIDE.md → "Interview Preparation"

Key questions to practice:
- Tell me about your background
- Why are you switching to data?
- Walk me through your anomaly detection project
- What's your experience with Python/ML?
- What's your biggest weakness?
- Tell me about a failure
- Why should we hire you as entry-level?

Spend 30 minutes on each. Record yourself. Review.
```

#### 3. Prepare Your Talking Points
```
Memorize:
- 30-second background pitch
- Why 12-year SOC experience is valuable
- Real impact of your projects (with numbers)
- How you learned (by doing, not just courses)
- What you're learning next
- Why this company specifically

Practice until you can say it naturally
```

#### 4. Prepare Questions to Ask Them
```
Good questions show interest:
- What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- What's the biggest challenge you're facing with data right now?
- How does this team work with other departments?
- What tools/stack do you use for data analysis?
- How will my background in security add value here?
- What's the learning culture like?

This shows you've thought deeply, not just read job posting
```
